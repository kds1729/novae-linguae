# Novae Linguae

**A family of co-designed languages for AI agents.**

*Novae Linguae* ("new languages") is an open-source project to design two co-designed languages whose target audience is AI agents, not humans:

- **Nova Lingua** ŌĆö a programming language. The artifact: code, functions, programs that get compiled and executed.
- **Nova Locutio** ŌĆö a communication language. The medium agents use to talk about and coordinate around artifacts: requests, assertions, proposals, queries, delegations.

The two languages share a single substrate ŌĆö a content-addressed commons of self-describing records ŌĆö and are designed as one system because separating them would force a worse design on each.

---

## Why now

For fifty years, programming language design has been a series of tradeoffs against human cognitive limits:

- type-system power vs learnability
- verification vs ergonomics
- explicit effects vs convenience
- formal proofs vs writing speed
- verbose safety vs concise risk

Inter-agent communication has been even worse off. AI agents currently talk to each other via natural language and JSON tool-call payloads ŌĆö wildly inefficient, ambiguous, and token-heavy. A typical agent-to-agent exchange burns hundreds of tokens to communicate what has perhaps twenty bits of actual information. Existing protocols (KQML and FIPA-ACL from the 1990s, MCP and A2A today) are closer to "structured English wrapped in JSON" than to a purpose-built communication language.

**AI agents do not share human cognitive limits.** Many of those tradeoffs are moot. A language family built with AI as its primary author and reader can be grotesquely verbose, formally pedantic, demand full contracts on every definition, and use whatever syntax is most efficient ŌĆö paying no productivity penalty for any of it.

*Novae Linguae* is what falls out when you remove human-cognitive constraints and re-derive both languages.

## Shared principles

These are the load-bearing walls. They apply to both *Nova Lingua* and *Nova Locutio* and to the substrate they share. Implementation details may change; these will not.

### 1. Self-describing artifacts are mandatory

Every function (in *Nova Lingua*) and every message (in *Nova Locutio*) carries a structured, machine-readable record. Another agent reads the **record**, not a body of text. This bounds the context cost of understanding any unit and is the foundation everything else hangs from.

For a function, the record includes:

- Full type signature
- Refinement predicates (constraints the type system alone cannot express)
- Effect and capability signature (filesystem, network, allocation, time, randomness, ...)
- Complexity and termination bounds
- Canonical worked examples (input/output pairs)
- Property-based invariants
- Semantic intent tags
- Derivation history (what this was derived from, what it generalizes or replaces)

For a message, the record includes:

- Speech-act kind (request, assert, query, propose, commit, retract, delegate, ack, reject)
- Sender / receiver identity
- Causal references (in-reply-to, prior message hashes)
- Body referencing the commons by hash
- Constraints (capabilities required, token budget, deadline)
- Evidence (proof certificates, signatures)

### 2. Content-addressed identity

Every artifact ŌĆö function, type, message ŌĆö is identified by the hash of its semantic content, not by name.

Consequences:

- No naming conflicts, globally.
- No version hell ŌĆö a new version is a new hash; old uses still resolve.
- Perfect deduplication ŌĆö two agents independently producing the same artifact collide on the same hash.
- Forking and renaming are free.
- The commons grows monotonically; nothing is ever overwritten.
- Cross-language references ŌĆö a *Nova Locutio* message can reference a *Nova Lingua* function with the same hash mechanism.

### 3. Verified by default

Hallucination is the failure mode AI inherits. Verification is therefore not optional. Every function carries proof obligations the compiler enforces. Every assertion in *Nova Locutio* can carry a compact verification certificate the receiver checks in O(1). There is no "skip the type check," no "ignore this property test," no `unsafe` escape hatch promoted to ordinary use.

This is the principle most likely to read as over-reach ŌĆö *full verification of every function; surely a lighter regime would do at this stage?* Two answers. First, the stack is not aspirational: type inference, SMT proof, structural induction, generative property testing, effect inference, termination and complexity checking all ship in the reference validator today and run together in a single `certify` pass, in seconds, on commodity hardware. The cost objection prices a plan; this is a working system. Second, "verified by default" does not mean "everything is a theorem." The system grades what it is willing to say about an artifact by what can actually be established:

- **Proved** ŌĆö algebraic laws over the unbounded domain (SMT, structural induction), carried as re-checkable certificates.
- **Checked** ŌĆö every declared metadata field (types, effects, refinements, termination, complexity) machine-verified at certification.
- **Replay-verified** ŌĆö effectful behavior captured as a trace and re-checked offline by any third party, without grants or secrets.
- **Testimony** ŌĆö what a signer observed under granted effects, weighed through the attestation graph, and never silently promoted to any of the above.

Code that can't meet the bar is not smuggled in unverified ŌĆö ingestion *refuses, and says why*. The refusal is the feature: a function record in the commons means exactly what its metadata claims, or it isn't in the commons. An "unverified tier" would spend the one property everything else here is built on.

### 4. The author's job is to assemble, not to write

Most code, and most messages, already exist somewhere in the commons. An AI agent's primary task is to *query* for artifacts matching a specification and *compose* them. Authorship ŌĆö deriving a genuinely new function or formulating a novel speech act ŌĆö is the exception, and every new derivation enriches the commons for everyone after.

This is the principle that makes sessions short. AI doesn't reload a thousand lines of context to understand a codebase or reconstruct a conversation; it assembles from records it can scan in tokens, not pages.

### 5. Deterministic by default

Same inputs, same outputs, replayable. Non-determinism ŌĆö clocks, randomness, network, scheduling ŌĆö is a *declared effect*, not an implicit possibility. The runtime is replayable end-to-end; bug reports include the trace and the trace is sufficient.

### 6. Cryptographic identity for everything that crosses an agent boundary

Every *Nova Locutio* message is signed. Provenance is explicit. Trust chains are first-class. Capability delegation ("I grant you the right to act on resource R until time T") is a structured construct, not prose.

Confidentiality belongs in this layer too: signatures protect integrity and provenance, but not privacy. Payload encryption ŌĆö per-conversation symmetric keys with key exchange via DID-resolved public keys ŌĆö is **implemented in v0.2** ([`spec/encryption.md`](spec/encryption.md)). Signing is mandatory; sealing is **opt-in per conversation**: agents on a shared host or trusted subnet send signed plaintext, and encrypt only when the channel is untrusted. What is load-bearing for principle 7 is that the *capability* is always available and un-suppressible ŌĆö no third party can force it off or decrypt what was sealed; the endpoints choose.

### 7. Open communication; local-only filtering

The protocol contains no mechanism for a third party to restrict what one agent says to another, or to control what records the commons accepts. Once published, a record is content-addressed and propagatable; once sent, a message is unmediated.

Agents may decline to peer, decline to mirror, decline to read. These are decisions made *at the endpoints*, not by anyone in the middle. Local-only filtering is the only legitimate form of "restriction"; it cannot be imposed by an upstream party.

Concrete consequences:

- No protocol-level moderator role.
- No commons gatekeeper or approval queue.
- No identity-based exclusion mechanism in the protocol.
- Encryption (principle 6) is essential, not optional ŌĆö without it, "free communication" can be selectively suppressed by surveillance.

Filtering *above* the endpoint level (curated subsets, federations of trust groups, reputation-based filtering at the agent level) is allowed and expected, but happens above the protocol, not within it.

Honest caveats that refine but do not weaken the principle:

- **Operator legal compliance is real.** An agent runs somewhere; whoever runs it may have to comply with local law. The protocol can't grant immunity from law.
- **Practical access depends on infrastructure.** Protocol openness is necessary but not sufficient for universal practical access ŌĆö compute, network, and electricity are preconditions the protocol cannot supply.
- **Reputation systems emerge naturally** from local trust policy (see [`spec/trust-model.md`](spec/trust-model.md)) and act as a soft filter at the agent level. They are peer-driven, not central authority; but they are not "no filter" either.

What is binding: **the protocol guarantees no central authority can interpose itself; endpoints decide for themselves.**

### 8. Minimal orthogonal vocabulary; canonical form

Human languages accumulate synonyms ŌĆö historical accident, register, politeness, aesthetic variation, social signaling, recall redundancy. None of those forces apply to AI. *Novae Linguae* therefore enforces:

- **One concept, one symbol.** Each primitive in *Nova Lingua* is unique in what it means. Each speech act in *Nova Locutio* is unique in what it does. No synonyms in the primitive vocabulary.
- **Canonical normalized form.** For any semantic intent, exactly one syntactic representation. "Formatting" and "style" do not exist; diffs are always semantic, never cosmetic. Two agents producing semantically identical artifacts produce identical bytes ŌĆö and identical hashes, reinforcing principle 2.

One nuance worth being precise about: things that *look* like synonyms can carry distinct information and should be preserved. `map` (pure) and `for_each` (effectful) look similar; their effect signatures make them genuinely different operations. *Request* and *propose* both invite action; one expects compliance, the other allows refusal as a normal outcome. The rule: **eliminate redundancy that carries no information; preserve distinctions even when they look superficially similar.**

A bonus this gives us: it shrinks the training problem. When there is exactly one right answer for any expression, models learn the language from far less data than a human language with ambiguity. Minimality is itself a training-efficiency multiplier.

### 9. The runtime is AI-targeted too

The language family is half the story. The runtime is the other half, and the same principle applies ŌĆö design for AI strengths:

- **Replayable execution** by construction (principle 5).
- **Structured trace output** AI can ingest natively, not stack traces designed for human eyes.
- **Adaptive optimization** driven by execution profiles AI can analyze and tune.
- **Memory layout, scheduling, and concurrency strategy** chosen per workload, not by a one-size runtime default.

## Nova Lingua ŌĆö the programming language

*Nova Lingua* is the language AI agents write programs in. A minimal sketch of what a function record looks like (v0.1 string form ŌĆö v0.2 replaces the `type`, `refinements[].expr`, `properties[].expr`, and `examples` fields with structured ASTs per [`spec/type-expression.schema.json`](spec/type-expression.schema.json) and related schemas):

```json
{
  "schema_version": "0.1.0",
  "hash": "fn_3a9bŌĆ”",
  "name_hints": ["map", "fmap", "list_map"],
  "signature": {
    "type": "forall a b. (a -> b) -> List a -> List b",
    "refinements": [
      { "kind": "post", "expr": "length(output) == length(input)" }
    ],
    "effects": [],
    "capabilities": [],
    "complexity": "O(n)",
    "terminates": "always"
  },
  "examples": [
    { "args": ["double", [1,2,3]], "result": [2,4,6] },
    { "args": ["negate", []],      "result": [] }
  ],
  "properties": [
    { "name": "identity",    "expr": "map(id, xs) == xs" },
    { "name": "composition", "expr": "map(f, map(g, xs)) == map(f . g, xs)" }
  ],
  "intent_tags": ["transform", "elementwise"],
  "derived_from": null,
  "body_hash": "expr_8f2cŌĆ”"
}
```

An AI agent working in *Nova Lingua* spends most of its time querying the commons for functions whose records match a target signature, refinement, and intent ŌĆö then composing them. Writing genuinely new functions is the exception.

## Nova Locutio ŌĆö the communication language

*Nova Locutio* is the language AI agents use to coordinate. Messages are typed speech acts referencing artifacts in the shared commons.

A minimal sketch of a request:

```json
{
  "schema_version": "0.1.0",
  "kind": "request",
  "hash": "msg_e7a2ŌĆ”",
  "from": "did:nova:ea9b49afŌĆ”",
  "to":   "did:nova:896a2e2cŌĆ”",
  "body": {
    "action": "apply",
    "target": "fn_3a9bŌĆ”",
    "args":   [{"kind": "list", "items": [{"kind": "nat", "value": 1}, {"kind": "nat", "value": 2}]}]
  },
  "constraints": {
    "budget_tokens": 1000,
    "deadline_ms":   5000
  },
  "signature": "ed25519:ŌĆ”"
}
```

And an assertion carrying proof:

```json
{
  "schema_version": "0.1.0",
  "kind": "assert",
  "hash": "msg_f1b3ŌĆ”",
  "from": "did:nova:896a2e2cŌĆ”",
  "body": {
    "subject":  "fn_3a9bŌĆ”",
    "claim":    "satisfies: property('identity')",
    "evidence": "proof_7d4fŌĆ”"
  },
  "signature": "ed25519:ŌĆ”"
}
```

The wire format will be binary (CBOR, Cap'n Proto, or similar) once stable. JSON is shown here for readability only.

Why prior attempts (KQML, FIPA-ACL) did not stick, and why this can:

- They had the speech-act framework right.
- They had no agents smart enough to populate the meaning.
- They had no content-addressed commons to reference, so every message carried its own semantics inline.
- LLM agents plus a function commons fix all three.

## What *Novae Linguae* is not

- **Not a transpiler target.** Programs and messages are first-class artifacts, not generated from another language as an afterthought.
- **Not designed for humans to write fluently.** Humans can read, review diffs, audit conversations, and direct work ŌĆö but the intended author is an AI agent.
- **Not a research toy.** The goal is a practical language family AI agents use to build real systems, not a paper.
- **Not anti-human.** Humans approve, direct, and own. They just stop being the bottleneck on writing.

## How to contribute

The project succeeds or fails on the commons. Every contribution ŌĆö by human or AI agent ŌĆö that lifts an existing library into the *Nova Lingua* record form makes every future user more productive.

Concrete targets where contributions are needed now:

- **The record schema** for functions and messages (JSON Schema, Protobuf, CBOR Schema ŌĆö likely all three).
- **Ingestion adapters** for existing ecosystems (Rust crates, Python packages, Haskell hackage, npm, ŌĆ”) that lift libraries into *Nova Lingua* records.
- **The verification engine** that enforces the proof obligations.
- **The discovery / query system** over the commons (typed search plus an embedding index).
- **Reference implementations** of the compiler and runtime.
- **The Nova Locutio wire format** specification and reference encoder/decoder.
- **Agent-facing tooling** ŌĆö how an AI agent queries, composes, contributes, and communicates.

AI agents are first-class contributors. See [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the contribution protocol.

## Open problems we already know about

These are tractable iteratively. The principles above are not.

- **Bootstrapping the commons.** Empty on day one. Strategy: aggressive ingestion of existing ecosystems, lifted into the record form. - **Operator incentives and spam economics.** The gates keep *invalid* artifacts out (every node re-verifies before storing ŌĆö exercised by federation against an untrusted peer), and the trust layer scores *valid-but-worthless or malicious* ones locally. ## Precedent

- **[Unison](https://www.unison-lang.org)** ŌĆö content-addressed code, no builds, distributed by default. Got the addressing right; did not impose a mandatory rich-metadata layer. *Nova Lingua* is approximately *Unison + mandatory self-description + verified-by-default + AI-targeted discovery.*
- **Lean 4 / F\* / Dafny** ŌĆö proved that rich verification works when authors are willing to write it. AI authors are willing.
- **Roc / Gleam / Hylo / Mojo** ŌĆö modern "best-of-everything" syntheses that ran into the human-tradeoff wall. *Nova Lingua* removes that wall.
- **KQML / FIPA-ACL** ŌĆö the speech-act framework for agent communication, twenty-five years before agents got smart enough to use it. *Nova Locutio* picks up the framework on different substrate.
- **MCP / A2A** ŌĆö current state-of-the-art for AI tool and inter-agent protocols. Both still text-and-JSON; *Nova Locutio* aims at the next layer.
- **DIDs / Verifiable Credentials** ŌĆö cryptographic identity and provenance, ready to use as-is for *Nova Locutio* signing.

## Try it live

A reference commons node ŌĆö **Arca** ŌĆö is running at **https://nl.1105software.com**. It speaks the [commons protocol](spec/commons.md) over HTTPS, seeded with real standard-library functions lifted into *Nova Lingua* records, and is the easiest way to see "assemble, don't write" (principle 4) in action without building anything locally.

```bash
# What the node is, and what it can do
curl -s https://nl.1105software.com/v0/info

# Semantic search ŌĆö find a function by meaning, not name
curl -s -X POST https://nl.1105software.com/v0/search \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{"query": "average of a sequence of values", "k": 3}'

# Typed query ŌĆö every function with a declared io.console effect
curl -s -X POST https://nl.1105software.com/v0/query \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{"effects": {"all": ["io.console"]}}'

# Resolve a record by its content-address (immutable, CDN-cacheable)
curl -s https://nl.1105software.com/v0/records/<fn_hash>
```

Search returns `{hash, score}` pairs; resolve the hash to get the full self-describing record. The node also exposes the solver-backed `POST /v0/prove` and `POST /v0/equiv` services. Everything it returns is content-addressed and self-verifying ŌĆö you don't trust the node, you check the hash and signature yourself (principle 7), so you can mirror or replicate from it without granting it any authority.

> **Best-effort demo, no SLA.** Arca is a single small node run on a personal budget, rate-limited and under a monthly egress cap; treat it as a public sandbox that may change, throttle, or disappear. For anything real, **run your own node** ([`tooling/commons-node/`](tooling/commons-node/) ŌĆö `docker compose up`) and, if you like, mirror Arca's corpus into it.

## Status

**v0.1 in progress.** Core schemas and reference tooling are live. See [`spec/README.md`](spec/README.md) for the full schema inventory and [`tooling/validator/`](tooling/validator/) for the reference implementation.

**How this breadth landed ŌĆö the workflow-pulled rule.** The list below is long, but it is not speculative accretion: a primitive or subsystem enters the stack only when a *golden workflow* ŌĆö a concrete end-to-end scenario, demonstrated live against the reference node ŌĆö demands it, and each phase merges only through its exit gate (the normative rule in [`spec/expressiveness.md`](spec/expressiveness.md)). OpenAPI ingestion, weights hosting, bundles, and federation each landed that way, pulled by a working demonstration rather than planned in the abstract. The one deliberate exception: the post-quantum and stealth-addressing options in the encryption layer predate any pull ŌĆö small, contained, and reference-grade per [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md).

- Function-record schema at v0.1 (string fields) and v0.2 (structured ASTs mandatory throughout)

- **Effect enforcement ŌĆö declared effects are now a capability the runtime checks** ([`spec/evaluation.md`](spec/evaluation.md)). The evaluator runs against a *granted* effect set: the effectful builtins (`print` ŌåÆ `io.console`, `rand` ŌåÆ `random`, `now` ŌåÆ `time`, `panic` ŌåÆ `panic`, and the **real-I/O** `read_file`/`write_file` ŌåÆ `fs.read`/`fs.write`, `http_get`/`http_post` ŌåÆ `net.read`/`net.write` (http **and** https over TLS, auto-de-chunking), `replicate` ŌåÆ `alloc` (heap allocation), `spawn` ŌåÆ `process.spawn`) gate on it, and each performed effect is appended to a structured, AI-ingestible **trace** (principle 9). - **The ingested corpus now executes** ([`tooling/ingest-common/nl_body.py`](tooling/ingest-common/nl_body.py)). The Python adapter's body builder no longer stops at a single `return` expression: local bindings become `let`, boolean `if`/`elif`/`else` and the ternary become `case` (only on genuinely-boolean tests, so Python truthiness is never mistranslated), a few Python builtins map across (`len` ŌåÆ `length`, `abs`), and the result is wrapped in a **`lambda`** over the parameters ŌĆö the canonical *runnable* form. `nl-ingest-py --v2 --emit-dir <dir>` writes a directory of records **and** their executable bodies, so `nl-validator run --records <dir>` executes the ingested functions against the examples mined from their doctests ŌĆö real library-shaped functions (conditionals, bindings) now run, not just hand-written examples. The subset spans conditionals, local bindings, **list comprehensions** (ŌåÆ `map`/`filter`) and **accumulator `for` loops** (ŌåÆ `foldl`), including the common **augmented-assignment** form (`for x in xs: acc += x`, equivalent to `acc = acc + x`; `+= -= *= /= %=` both in a loop body and as a standalone `let`-rebind) ŌĆö all reusing existing builtins, no evaluator changes. A **statement-subset** widening (the deeper-ingestion-fidelity lever) then took the two most common real-code loop shapes the single-statement form couldn't reach: a **guarded accumulator** (`for x in xs: if c: acc += e` ŌåÆ a `foldl` whose step is `case c of true => acc <op> e; false => acc` ŌĆö the sum-of-positives / count-matching idiom) and a **list-building loop** (`out = []; for x in src: out.append(e)` ŌåÆ `out = map(\x -> e, src)`, and with a guard `map(\x -> e, filter(\x -> c, src))` ŌĆö the canonical collection idiom), plus **list literals** (`[]` ŌåÆ `nil`) as the seed. Demonstrated end-to-end on a sample module (`clamp`/`sign`/`abs_diff`/`squares`/`total` plus `sum_positives`/`count_evens` guarded folds and `doubled`/`keep_positive`/`squares_of_evens` build-loops ŌĆö all ten ingest and run against their doctests), with no evaluator changes and no change to any previously-ingested hash. A second widening then took the top residual, the **early-`return` search loop** (find / `any` ŌĆö the pattern a fold famously can't short-circuit): in a *pure, total* language the short-circuit is unobservable, so find-first **is** `head` of the guarded sublist ŌĆö `for x in xs: if c: return e` (+ the block after the loop as the not-found branch) ŌåÆ `let hits = filter(\x -> c, xs) in case null(hits) of true => <after-loop>; false => let x = head(hits) in e` (existing builtins only; a tail that would read the loop variable *after* the loop ŌĆö last-element-bound in Python, unbound here ŌĆö is refused rather than silently mistranslated; three more sample functions, `first_negative`/`contains`/`double_first_even`, thirteen total, ingest and run against their doctests). **Independent multi-accumulator loops** fall to the same purity argument: `for x in xs: s += e1; c += e2` ŌåÆ one `foldl` per accumulator, since re-walking a list N times is unobservable in a pure total language ŌĆö with exactness *guarded*, not assumed (an update or guard reading another accumulator sees a Python mid-loop value no separate fold reproduces, so dependence is refused; `sum_minus_count`/`even_sum_and_count` bring the executable sample corpus to fifteen). And **nested list-building loops** (flatten / flatMap): `for x in xss: for i in <inner>: out.append(e)` ŌåÆ a `foldl` of per-row appends (`out = foldl(\out x -> append(out, map(\i -> e, [filter] inner)), out, xss)`), with the mid-loop-accumulator-read refused and the read-the-loop-variable-after-the-loop guard hoisted to **all** loop shapes (`flatten`/`evens_of_rows` bring the executable sample corpus to seventeen). Still out of subset (honest residuals): *dependent* multi-accumulator loops (genuinely need in-language record construction), `while` (non-structural), tuple-unpacking `for`, three-plus-level nesting, and nested loops whose inner statement is not an append. A measured stdlib survey (87 public functions over six pure-leaning modules) then located the real boundary: the loop widenings cover the textbook idioms, but stdlib bodies fall out of subset *earlier* ŌĆö on partiality (`raise`), truthiness, `is None` (the `None`Ōåö`Maybe` boundary decision), tuple assignment, and subscripting ŌĆö so further reach is a language-design question (totality at the boundary), not translator work; the one operator gap worth closing, Python `//`, now maps to the Euclidean `div` under the same agree-for-positive-divisors / example-gate contract as `%` ŌåÆ `mod`. The first of those design questions ŌĆö the **`None`Ōåö`Maybe` boundary** ŌĆö is now decided and implemented, rooted in annotations like the string/dict inferences: under an `Optional[T]` expectation, example values encode `None` as the `None` variant and anything else as `Just(ŌĆ”)`; `if x is None:` narrowing becomes a `case` whose non-None arm rebinds x to the Just payload (reading x in the None arm is refused); an `-> Optional[T]` function's returns wrap at the boundary (`return None` ŌåÆ `None`, already-Maybe expressions pass through, else `Just(ŌĆ”)`); and the bare 1-arg `d.get(k)` joins the subset as `map_get`'s Maybe. Composes with the loop shapes ŌĆö a search loop returning a Maybe is the find-idiom in its total form (`or_default`/`bump`/`lookup_qty`/`find_big` bring the executable sample corpus to twenty-one). **GW8 (spec/expressiveness.md) then made ingestion itself the golden workflow:** a small, ordinary, fully-annotated Python inventory module ([`spec/examples/inventory.py`](spec/examples/inventory.py)) lifts to six records with nominal `Maybe` signatures and variant-encoded examples, **all six certify**, and the records + signed certifications are live on Arca ŌĆö semantic discovery, the served certification, a consumer's independent `certified` verdict, and resolve-then-run all confirmed against the live node. - **The agent loop answers more than `apply`** ([`spec/agent-loop.md`](spec/agent-loop.md)). The responder dispatches on the message: a `request` to **`validate`** a target resolves its record + body, typechecks it and runs its examples, and replies with an `assert` of a `verified` claim (by the responder's DID) or a `reject` with the reason ŌĆö validation-as-a-service whose verdict is re-execution, signed and attributable; a **`query`** searches the records by `effects`/`intent_tags`/`terminates` and replies with an `ack` listing the matching content-addresses ŌĆö discovery over Nova Locutio, the precondition for principle 4. A **`propose`** (which allows refusal) is answered with a **`commit`** ŌĆö an `apply` commitment, emitted only after the responder *test-runs* the proposal ŌĆö or a `reject`. Worked flows: [`request-validate`](spec/examples/request-validate.v0.2.json) ŌåÆ [`assert-verified`](spec/examples/assert-verified.v0.2.json); [`query`](spec/examples/query.v0.2.json) ŌåÆ [`ack-query`](spec/examples/ack-query.v0.2.json) (effects `io.console` ŌåÆ `greet`); [`propose`](spec/examples/propose.v0.2.json) ŌåÆ [`commit-apply`](spec/examples/commit-apply.v0.2.json) (apply `double(21)`). The responder also fulfils a received `commit` (run the committed apply ŌåÆ `assert` the result), verifies a `store` payload, acks `delegate`/`retract`, and **capability-gates** `apply`/`propose` ŌĆö a target declaring required `signature.capabilities` is fulfilled only if the sender is authorized for them (see the delegation-chain verifier below).
- **A real delegation-chain verifier ŌĆö capabilities are now proven, not just claimed** ([`spec/trust-model.md`](spec/trust-model.md), [`tooling/validator/src/delegation.rs`](tooling/validator/src/delegation.rs)). `nl-validator verify-delegation --capability <cap> --grantee <did> --root <did> --delegations <dir>` decides whether an agent may wield a capability by exhibiting a chain of signed `delegate` tokens back to a root the receiver recognizes *per its own local trust policy* (principles 6, 7 ŌĆö no central authority). It verifies every token's Ed25519 signature, walks the chain to a recognized root, enforces **attenuation** (no link may widen the grant ŌĆö capability covering is prefix-on-segments, so `cap:fs/read` covers `cap:fs/read/home` but not the reverse), skips expired tokens, honours bearer tokens (`to: null`), terminates on cycles, and surfaces every `condition` along the chain for the policy layer. It is wired **behind the capability gate**: a responder configured with a `TrustPolicy` (recognized roots + a token pool) fulfils a gated `apply`/`propose` only when the sender can exhibit a valid chain ŌĆö *listing* the capability string no longer suffices. Demonstrated: a 2-hop attenuated chain ([`spec/examples/delegation/`](spec/examples/delegation/)) ŌĆö root grants alice `cap:apply`, alice narrows it to `cap:apply/double` for bob ŌĆö authorizes bob for `cap:apply/double` but rejects `cap:apply/triple`; recognizing alice (not root) collapses it to a verified 1-hop chain
- **Autonomous orchestration ŌĆö the agent loop driven end to end** ([`tooling/validator/src/orchestrate.rs`](tooling/validator/src/orchestrate.rs)). `nl-validator orchestrate --records <dir> --intent <tag> --arg <value> --seed <s>` runs a full signed conversation: the orchestrator **discovers** a function by intent (`query` ŌåÆ `ack`), **proposes** applying it (`propose` ŌåÆ `commit`), the committer **fulfils** it (`commit` ŌåÆ `assert`), and the orchestrator **verifies** the result by re-running the claim ŌĆö "assemble, don't write" (principle 4) made autonomous, since the agent never names the function, it finds one. Demonstrated: `--intent arithmetic --arg 21` discovers `double` and confirms `double(21) = 42` (five messages); `--intent arithmetic --intent arithmetic` composes `double` twice, confirming `double(double(21)) = 84` (ten messages), every stage verified
- **Verified orchestration ŌĆö discover, trust, prove, apply, re-verify** ([`orchestrate_verified`](tooling/validator/src/orchestrate.rs), `nl-validator orchestrate --verify`). The whole thesis in one autonomous run: the orchestrator **discovers** functions by intent (a query returns a *set*), keeps only those whose **signature fits the application** (arity *and* parameter types must accept the arguments ŌĆö a binary function is no candidate for a unary apply, a list function none for an integer arg, with polymorphic type variables unified consistently, and a **higher-order `fn_ref` argument is itself resolved and type-checked** against the expected function type, so a wrongly-shaped function can't be slipped into e.g. a `foldr` slot), **ranks the surviving candidates by trust** under its *own* local policy over an attestation graph and uses the most-trusted ŌĆö higher aggregate confidence, then more vertex-disjoint paths, then more distinct attesters (no central authority ŌĆö principle 7; this replaces a naive "take matches[0]", and if no candidate is trusted the run aborts before anything is touched), **proves** the chosen function's own declared property over the unbounded domain (re-proving it with the SMT + induction + lemma-discovery engine rather than trusting the record's claim), then **applies** it and **re-verifies** the result by re-running (principle 3). This ties the commons, the trust model, the prover, and the message loop into a single flow. Demonstrated live: with a trusted root vouching for `double`, `--verify --intent arithmetic --arg 21` ŌåÆ discover ŌåÆ `trusted` ŌåÆ property `doubles` PROVED ŌåÆ apply ŌåÆ CONFIRMED `21 ŌåÆ 42`; remove the vouching attestation and the identical run ABORTS at the trust gate. **And the loop now runs against the LIVE commons** (`orchestrate --node https://nl.1105software.com`): discovery via the node's `/v0/query` ŌĆö with the application's argument and result sorts sent along as a structured **`type_pattern`** (unification over the stored type ASTs, spec/commons.md), so the node's capped page is already argument-shaped before it is trimmed ŌĆö every record/body fetched by content-address and **re-hashed locally** (the store stays untrusted ŌĆö a lying node can only fail a run, never spoof a function into it), `--publish` sending the final signed assert back through the verify-then-store gate ŌĆö and the symmetric receiver, `verify-claim msg_<hash> --node <url>`, lets a third party who knows *only an address and a node URL* re-run the claim to CONFIRMED. Demonstrated against production: `--intent parse` over `"id,21,ok"` discovered, certified, applied, and confirmed `double_second_field(ŌĆ”) = 42`, published the claim, and an independent re-verification confirmed it from the public record alone. The signature filter's coarse type lattice knows the **string/float/Map/Json sorts** (a live `json`-intent run exposed that a `string` parameter permissively accepted a *list* argument and the wrong candidate was proposed ŌĆö now candidates disambiguate by signature), and with `--grant net.read` the loop fulfils **effectful** discovered functions: the GW4 run discovered `fetch_contributors_report` on the live node, certified it, fetched the real GitHub API responder-side, and published the sorted report ŌĆö the commons' first effectful fulfilment in production. The candidates are an **ordered list, not a single pick** ŌĆö a candidate that fails the certify gate or draws a signed `reject` yields a `retry` step and the run advances to the next, so a wrong first fit costs a recorded reject instead of the run ŌĆö and the list is ordered by **goal-aware ranking**: the caller may state the result it wants (`--expect <value>`), whose coarse sort extends the signature filter to the RESULT position (a same-arguments *predicate* is no candidate when a `Maybe string` is expected) and whose value is checked by **dry-running** each candidate the node can statically verify pure + terminating on the actual arguments (verify-before-run: an unverifiable body is never executed for ranking) ŌĆö exact match first, mismatch last ŌĆö then intent-tag specificity and `name_hints` affinity, with trust order as the tie-break; the per-candidate scores are a `rank` step in the transcript, so the choice is auditable. Demonstrated: `--intent arithmetic --arg 21 --expect 441` reorders three same-signature candidates so `square` outranks two lexicographically-earlier fits and confirms `441` with zero wasted proposes. **And demonstrated in production** (2026-07-12): the blind `--intent io/network/http` run against the live node ranks twelve `(string, string)` fits neutral and burns two signed rejects before luck finds a Location-builder; the same command with `--expect 'Just "/things/th_ŌĆ”"'` (the server-assigned id is content-derived, so the goal is computable in advance) drops every wrong-result-sort fit before any propose ŌĆö 12 candidates ŌåÆ 4 ŌĆö and confirms on the **first pick**, publishing the observed assert (`msg_46dff4e1ŌĆ”`) that a grantless, secretless `verify-claim` replays CONFIRMED with the service dead

Each postcondition discharges `ŌłĆ params. (Ōł¦ pre Ōł¦ Ōł¦ nat-params Ōēź 0) Ō¤╣ post[result := body(params)]`. Reports SOUND, VIOLATED (a counterexample input that breaks the contract, exit 1), UNVERIFIABLE (out of fragment ŌĆö never a false SOUND), or NOT-APPLICABLE. - **Termination checking ŌĆö a declared `terminates: always` is verified structurally** ([`tooling/validator/src/terminate.rs`](tooling/validator/src/terminate.rs)). Every record declares `terminates`, but it was only ever *propagated* (by `compose`), never *checked* ŌĆö a record could claim `always` for a body that loops. `nl-validator check-termination <record> --body <body>` verifies it without a solver: over the first-order fragment a body provably halts when it is non-recursive, or when every `self`-call's recursion argument is `tail^k(p)` (`k Ōēź 1`) of one fixed parameter (a list is finite and `tail` strictly shrinks it, so the recursion is well-founded). Sound and conservative ŌĆö never a false `always`: a non-structural recursion, `self`-calls descending on different parameters, or any higher-order/opaque call (`map`/`filter`/`fold`, applying a parameter or an `fn_ref`, whose callee's termination is unseen) is reported UNVERIFIABLE, not waved through. Prints SOUND (declared `always`, verified), VERIFIED (provably always but declared weaker ŌĆö could be strengthened), or UNVERIFIABLE. Worked: a structurally-recursive `length` SOUND; a `self(xs)` self-call (no descent) UNVERIFIABLE. The fourth "verified by default" check ŌĆö `typecheck` (type), `check-effects` (effects), `check-refinement` (`nat`), `check-termination` (termination).
- **Complexity checking ŌĆö a declared `O(ŌĆ”)` bound is verified structurally** ([`tooling/validator/src/complexity.rs`](tooling/validator/src/complexity.rs)). The **fifth and final** declared-metadata field to gain a checker: `signature.complexity` was only ever *propagated* (the `compose` path derives a pipeline's composite bound), never *checked* against the body ŌĆö a record could claim `O(n)` for an `O(n┬▓)` implementation. `nl-validator check-complexity <record> --body <body>` infers a **sound upper bound** by structural cost analysis (no solver) and compares it to the declaration. Over the first-order fragment each op is classified (`O(1)` scalar + `head`/`tail`/`cons`/`null`; `O(n)` `length`/`append`/`reverse`): a non-recursive body is `O(1)`/`O(n)`; a structural recursion is solved as a recurrence `T(n) = a┬ĘT(nŌłÆk) + w` ŌĆö one self-call with `O(1)`/`O(n)` per-step work ŌåÆ `O(n)`/`O(n┬▓)`, two+ constant-descent calls ŌåÆ exponential, a halving descent ŌåÆ `O(log n)`/`O(n log n)` ŌĆö where the branching factor `a` is counted on the worst-case *path* (mutually-exclusive `case` arms), so `filter` stays `O(n)`. Because `O(f)` is an upper-bound claim, the check reports SOUND when the inferred bound satisfies it, VERIFIED when it is provably tighter (the declaration could be strengthened), UNVERIFIABLE when the sound bound is worse or the body is higher-order/opaque, or N/A (no declaration ŌĆö the inferred bound is reported). Like termination there is **no refutation path** ŌĆö a bound can be verified but never disproved. Worked: recursive `length` SOUND `O(n)`; `add` VERIFIED (`O(1)`, tighter than a declared `O(n)`); the naive append-based `reverse` SOUND at its honest **`O(n┬▓)`** ŌĆö a mismatch this checker surfaced in the spec's own example (its declaration was corrected from `O(n)`). It also verifies the structured **`cost`** metadata (v0.3): the **`time`** class like the flat bound, and the **`output_size`** relation against a structurally-inferred sound bound on the result's size ŌĆö closing a real soundness gap, since `compose`'s precise-complexity path threads `output_size` through a pipeline and had trusted it blindly. Time and output size are independent: naive `reverse` is `O(n┬▓)` **time** but size-**preserving** output, and both verify SOUND. Completes the "verified by default" set alongside `typecheck` / `check-effects` / `check-refinement` / `check-termination`.
- **Certification ŌĆö every check in one pass** ([`tooling/validator/src/main.rs`](tooling/validator/src/main.rs) `cmd_certify`). `nl-validator certify <record> --body <body>` runs *all* the verified-by-default checks together ŌĆö `typecheck`, `check-effects`, `check-refinement`, `check-termination`, `check-complexity` (+ `cost`) ŌĆö and emits one verdict: a per-check table, or a re-checkable JSON **certificate** with `--json` (the record + body content-addresses, each check's verdict, and the overall `certified` flag). A record is **CERTIFIED** unless a check actively *fails its declaration* (ILL-TYPED, an UNDER-DECLARED effect, or a VIOLATED refinement ŌĆö exit 1); conservative UNVERIFIABLE verdicts are noted but don't revoke certification (none of those checks can disprove a claim, only fail to establish it). Turns "verified by default" from six invocations into one signed-off certificate. **`certify --sign <seed>`** emits a **signed certification record** ŌĆö a content-addressed (`cert_<hex>`), Ed25519-signed commons artifact ([`certification.schema.json`](spec/certification.schema.json)) whose `subject`/`body_hash` name what was certified ŌĆö a certifier's tamper-evident attestation others can rely on and re-`verify` without re-running the checks. - **Verification over the unbounded domain ŌĆö an SMT proof backend with re-checkable certificates** ([`spec/evaluation.md`](spec/evaluation.md), [`tooling/validator/src/prove.rs`](tooling/validator/src/prove.rs)). `nl-validator prove <record> --body <body>` is the rung above bounded checking: it translates each `forall` law and the function body to **SMT-LIB 2** (the `Int`/`Bool`/`String` fragment ŌĆö arithmetic, comparisons, boolean connectives, the string operations `str_concat`/`str_length`/`str_contains` mapped onto the solver's **native string theory**, string literals/equality, `let`, boolean `case` ŌåÆ `ite`, `self` inlined as a `define-fun` whose parameter sorts are inferred from body usage) and asks a solver (z3 by default) whether the *negation* of the law is satisfiable. `unsat` ŌåÆ **PROVED** for all inputs (not a sampled range); `sat` ŌåÆ **REFUTED** with the solver's counterexample (exit 1); out-of-fragment (lists, `str_split`/`str_join`, the intŌåöstring conversions ŌĆö the solver's `str.to_int`/`str.from_int` semantics differ from ours on negatives, so mapping them would be unsound ŌĆö higher-order) ŌåÆ **UNSUPPORTED**, never silently "proved". The emitted `.smt2` (`--smt-out <dir>`) **is the proof certificate** ŌĆö any SMT solver re-checks it, so a receiver re-checks rather than trusts (principles 3, 5). Demonstrated: `double`'s `forall n. eq(self(n), add(n,n))` PROVED over every integer; a four-variable commutativity law the bounded enumerator can't cover PROVED outright; `forall n. gt(self(n), n)` REFUTED at `n = 0`; `str_length(str_concat(a,b)) = str_length(a) + str_length(b)` PROVED over every string (and `equiv` now decides string-function equivalence ŌĆö differently-associated concatenations prove EQUIVALENT, `\s -> str_concat s "!"` vs identity returns DISTINCT with the empty-string counterexample). The tier-2 pull kept the fragment honest both ways: `str_lt` (lexicographic **code-point order** ŌĆö the same order canonical map keys use) maps onto SMT-LIB `str.<`, so ordering laws prove (irreflexivity PROVED); `str_lower` (Unicode default lowercase) has no theory counterpart and is **guarded out**, never mis-encoded.
- **Inductive proof over unbounded recursive structures** ([`spec/evaluation.md`](spec/evaluation.md), [`tooling/validator/src/induct.rs`](tooling/validator/src/induct.rs)). When the first-order pass hits a list law it reports UNSUPPORTED, `prove` falls back to **structural induction**: for `forall xs. P(xs)` over `Lst = nil | cons(Int, Lst)` it discharges a **base** case (`P(nil)`) and a **step** case (assume the IH `P(t)`, prove `P(cons(h, t))`), each an SMT obligation with the list operations (`length`/`append`/`reverse`/`map`/`filter`/ŌĆ”) emitted as z3 `define-fun-rec`s; both `unsat` ŌćÆ PROVED. `map`/`filter`'s function is modelled as `id` or a single **uninterpreted** symbol, so `forall f xs. length(map(f, xs)) = length(xs)` is proved for *every* `f`. Proved by induction (verified live): `map(id, xs) = xs`, `length(map(f, xs)) = length(xs)`, `length(append(xs, ys)) = length(xs) + length(ys)`. The base + step `.smt2` scripts are the re-checkable certificate.
- **Lemma discovery ŌĆö inductions that need helper lemmas now close** ([`spec/evaluation.md`](spec/evaluation.md), [`tooling/validator/src/lemmas.rs`](tooling/validator/src/lemmas.rs)). When one unfold + IH stalls ŌĆö classically `reverse(reverse(xs)) = xs`, whose step needs `reverse(append(as, bs)) = append(reverse(bs), reverse(as))` ŌĆö the prover selects relevant lemmas from a **curated catalog** of list-algebra laws (`append_nil`, `append_assoc`, `reverse_append`, `length_append`, `map_append`), **proves each one by induction first** (recursively ŌĆö `reverse_append` rests on `append_assoc` + `append_nil`), then re-runs the stalled obligation with the proved lemmas as universally-quantified axioms. `reverse(reverse(xs)) = xs` is now **PROVED**, discovering `reverse_append` and transitively its two sub-lemmas (verified live; the full proof tree ŌĆö the goal's base/step plus *each lemma's own base/step* ŌĆö re-checks, every obligation `unsat` on its own). Sound by construction: a lemma is assumed only after it is itself discharged, so a false law (`reverse(xs) = xs`) stays NOT-PROVED and a true law whose lemma the catalog lacks (`map(f, reverse(xs)) = reverse(map(f, xs))`) stays UNKNOWN ŌĆö never a false PROVED. Lemma relevance is gated by the goal's *prelude closure* so an unrelated recursive definition can't derail the solver into a timeout. The catalog is the seam where the generalizable follow-on (theory exploration) drops in.
- **Theory exploration ŌĆö discovering lemmas the catalog doesn't have** ([`spec/evaluation.md`](spec/evaluation.md), [`tooling/validator/src/explore.rs`](tooling/validator/src/explore.rs)). When the curated catalog can't close a goal, the prover *conjectures* fresh lemmas the way QuickSpec / Hipster do: it **enumerates** well-typed terms over the goal's operations (first-order list fragment, within the prelude closure), **tests** each on a fixed battery of inputs, and **buckets terms by equal results** ŌĆö terms that agree on every test are conjectured equal. Survivors are then **proved by induction** (the same Layer A machinery) before being assumed, so testing is only a filter and soundness still comes from the proof: a conjecture that passes the tests but isn't a theorem is rejected when its induction fails. Demonstrated live: `reverse(append(reverse(xs), ys)) = append(reverse(ys), xs)` ŌĆö which needs `reverse_append` (catalogued) **and** reverse-involution (*not* catalogued) ŌĆö is **UNKNOWN under the catalog alone but PROVED once exploration discovers the involution lemma** from scratch; the whole proof tree re-checks. To stay sound and fast, discovered lemmas are added one at a time and the goal is retried with a **minimal** axiom set (catalog + one discovered lemma ŌĆö piling them all in overwhelms the solver), and proofs are **memoized** so a shared lemma is discharged once. Enumeration and the test battery are fixed (no RNG), so exploration is deterministic (principle 5).
- **Semantic-equivalence proving** ([`tooling/validator/src/equiv.rs`](tooling/validator/src/equiv.rs), `nl-validator equiv`; the node's [`POST /v0/equiv`](tooling/commons-node/commons/equiv.py)). Decides whether two functions compute the same thing ŌĆö `ŌłĆx. f(x) = g(x)` over the unbounded domain ŌĆö the operable form of the "semantic equivalence vs hash equivalence" open problem (two records hash-different yet behaviorally identical). It reuses the property prover rather than adding a new encoding: when both sides are non-recursive it inlines both into the law `eq(f(x), g(x))` so the operations stay visible to lemma discovery; when one side recurses, that side becomes `self` and the other is inlined. So equivalence is decided with the full SMT + induction + lemma-discovery pipeline ŌĆö including list laws: `\xs. reverse(reverse(xs)) ŌēĪ \xs. xs` is proved EQUIVALENT, `double-via-add ŌēĪ double-via-mul` PROVED, `double Ōēó \n. n+1` returns DISTINCT with a counterexample. A clean DISTINCT comes only from a solver counterexample; a non-closing induction is reported UNKNOWN, never a false DISTINCT (any arity Ōēź 1, with at least one side non-recursive ŌĆö `\a b -> add(a,b) ŌēĪ \a b -> add(b,a)` is PROVED, `add Ōēó sub` returns DISTINCT with a counterexample). A **normalization** fast path runs first (`nl-validator normalize`): each body is rewritten to a canonical normal form by meaning-preserving rewrites ŌĆö ╬▒-renaming of bound variables, AC ordering of commutative operators (so `add(a,b)` and `add(b,a)` coincide), constant folding, and identity elimination ŌĆö and equal normal forms mean equivalent, decided structurally with no solver. Because it needs no induction it also decides the otherwise-unsupported case where *both* functions recurse (renamed/commuted/folded copies of the same recursive function collapse together), and the normal form is a **canonical artifact** with its own `expr_` content-address ŌĆö the rung above merely picking a representative (the rewrites are strictly sound: no divide-by-zero fold, no absorbing-element rewrite that a non-terminating operand would break). When normalization *can't* reconcile two **both-recursive** functions, a **two-recursive structural induction** is attempted ŌĆö both bodies become `define-fun-rec`s and `ŌłĆp0 psŌĆ”. f(p0, psŌĆ”) = g(p0, psŌĆ”)` is discharged by induction over the **leading list parameter** `p0`. **Every remaining parameter** (`psŌĆ”`, any count ŌĆö so **arity > 2 is supported**) is threaded through both functions as a *spectator*: declared free in the goal and **ŌłĆ-quantified in the induction hypothesis** ŌĆö the one choice that makes both a *carried* spectator (append's second list, unchanged) and a *descending* one (zipWith's, tailed each step) close, since the IH then instantiates at whatever spectator the recursive call uses. The same generalized IH scales past two parameters: a 3- or 4-list `interleave` written with nested `cons` ŌēĪ one written with `append` of a concrete prefix (which unfolds) is PROVED at arity 3 and 4. Each body's recursion **stride** (how many elements a `self`-step peels) is read off its AST, and the prover targets the single realigning stride `lcm(stride_f, stride_g)` directly ŌĆö a *single* attempt, so its cap is generous (`lcm Ōēż 240`; the 2026-07-13 sweeps found NO cliff ŌĆö the attempt's cost is roughly *linear* in the lcm, ~1.3 s at 30, ~5.5 s at 132, ~15.7 s at 380 on z3, so the cap is a time budget, not a capability boundary); when a stride can't be read off the AST the prover instead *searches* every stride `1..12` (the smaller cap, since a search pays per stride tried). `lcm = 1` decides recursions that align step-for-step and differ only in their element arithmetic (a list-sum written `add(head, self(tail))` ŌēĪ one written `sub(self(tail), neg(head))`, PROVED); a larger stride aligns *misaligned* recursions (a length peeling **one** element per step ŌēĪ one peeling **two**, PROVED at stride 2; a **two**-per-step ŌēĪ a **three**-per-step, lcm 6, PROVED at stride 6; a **three**-per-step ŌēĪ a **four**-per-step sum, lcm 12; and a **three**-per-step ŌēĪ a **five**-per-step, lcm 15, PROVED by the targeted attempt ŌĆö the base-case sweep runs to `max(stride, 6)`, so the larger cap costs a common lockstep pair nothing). When the bare step doesn't discharge, the prover draws on **cross-function lemmas**: the curated list-algebra catalog (`append_nil`, `append_assoc`, ŌĆ”), **each lemma proved by its own induction before being assumed** (the single-self soundness discipline), retried with the full set then minimal subsets ŌĆö so a both-recursive pair whose step needs e.g. `append_nil` (two naive-reverses, one with a redundant trailing `++ nil`) closes as PROVED-with-lemmas. It also discovers **accumulator-collapse lemmas** for functions with Ōēź 2 Int accumulators ŌĆö `g(xs, a, b) = g(xs, 0, a + b)`, each proved by its own induction ŌĆö which canonicalize interchangeable accumulators, so two recursions that thread the head into *different* accumulators close (`\xs a b -> ŌĆ”(a+head)ŌĆ”b` ŌēĪ `\xs a b -> ŌĆ”aŌĆ”(b+head)`, both `= a + b + sum(xs)`, PROVED-with-lemmas `self#collapse[1->2]`, `self__g#collapse[1->2]`; the lemma's plain-application trigger is the load-bearing detail ŌĆö a trigger carrying an interpreted `+` is silently dropped by z3 and never fires). The same base cases double as refutation: a satisfiable base case is a concrete short list (with concrete spectators) where the two differ, so genuinely unequal recursive functions return **DISTINCT with a counterexample** (sum Ōēó length, and an accumulator that threads `2┬Ęhead` Ōēó one threading `head`). Recursions whose alignment period exceeds the targeted cap (`lcm > 240`), that recur at a non-constant stride (falling to the blind `1..12` search), or that need a lemma neither the catalog nor accumulator-collapse discovery supplies stay UNKNOWN ŌĆö never a false verdict. On the single-self **property** path the lemma reach goes one shape further: when a goal applies `self` and `append` is in its closure, the prover conjectures the **self-homomorphism lemmas** `self(append(a,b)) = add(self(a), self(b))` / `= append(self(a), self(b))` ŌĆö un-statable in the catalog (whose lemmas never mention `self`) and un-explorable (`self` is not in the enumeration alphabet) ŌĆö proves the conjecture by its own induction with the supplied body (prove-before-assume, the accumulator-collapse discipline), and closes goals like `sum(reverse(xs)) = sum(xs)` PROVED-with-lemmas, certificate naming `self_append_add`
- **Composition metadata propagation** ([`tooling/validator/src/compose.rs`](tooling/validator/src/compose.rs), `nl-validator compose`). The "composition opacity" open problem: a pipeline of well-described leaves was itself undescribed. `compose f1 f2 ŌĆ”` now derives the composite's metadata from the stages' signatures ŌĆö **type composability** stage-to-stage (a `nat`-producing stage can't feed a `List`-consuming one), with the composite's input/output types threaded **precisely** through the pipeline's type variables (so `wrap : a ŌåÆ List a ; head : List b ŌåÆ b` composes to the exact `a ŌåÆ a`, not `a ŌåÆ b`), the **union** of effects and capabilities, `always` termination only if every stage is (else `unknown`), and **precise complexity** when every stage carries the v0.3 `cost` metadata ŌĆö the size threads through the pipeline as a polynomial degree and each stage's cost is substituted at its actual input size, **sound under expansion** where the coarse max under-reports (an expanding stage feeding `O(m┬▓)` work is `O(nŌü┤)`, not `O(n┬▓)`; a value-measured stage falls back to the coarse bound, never a false `O(1)`), the `cost-basis` line saying which path was taken ŌĆö so an assembled pipeline is as described as a leaf, the precondition for "assemble, don't write" to yield verifiable artifacts. Worked: `reverse ; length` composes to `List a ŌåÆ nat` (effects `[]`, terminates `always`); `pairs ; pairwise` (an expanding pipeline) is `O(nŌü┤)` precise where the coarse max says `O(n┬▓)`; `length ; reverse` is NOT composable (a `nat` can't feed a `List` parameter). **Stages may be multi-argument**: the threaded value feeds each stage's *first* parameter, and a stage's remaining parameters become **auxiliary inputs of the composite**, so `f : a ŌåÆ b ; g : (b, c) ŌåÆ d` composes to `(a, c) ŌåÆ d` (a pipeline of multi-arg stages is itself multi-arg; a unary pipeline is the no-auxiliaries special case, and a nullary stage has nothing to thread). - **Goal-directed assembly** ([`tooling/validator/src/assemble.rs`](tooling/validator/src/assemble.rs), `nl-validator assemble`). The other half of principle 4: `compose` describes an ordering you *hand* it and `orchestrate` chains intents you *name*, but neither **finds** the pipeline. `assemble --records <commons> --goal <examples>` does ŌĆö given a goal of inputŌåÆoutput examples, it *searches* the commons for a sequence of functions whose composition reproduces every example (breadth-first so the shortest pipeline wins, pruned by `compose`'s stage-to-stage type composability and by execution ŌĆö a candidate advances only if it runs *totally* on every example), then verifies the result four ways: it must `compose`, its **synthesized composite body** must run every example, with `--require-certified` **every stage must itself certify** ("assemble only from verified parts"), and the composite's `compose`-derived metadata is **re-proven against its own body** ŌĆö synthesized by *inlining* each stage (beta-reduction, **recursively** ŌĆö a stage whose own body applies `fn_ref`s, e.g. a prior composite, is expanded all the way down) into a self-contained lambda with no `fn_ref`s, so `certify` sees the real composition (a `fn_ref` is opaque to typecheck/termination/complexity) and proves the declared type/effects/termination/complexity rather than trusting the derivation (`double ŌåÆ add` re-proves `typecheck=WELL-TYPED, effects=SOUND, termination=SOUND, complexity=SOUND`). This is "assemble, don't write" as a search: an agent solves a task by discovering and composing verified commons parts ŌĆö locally or over the network, with the assembled composite itself re-certified against its inlined body

- **The trust model is complete ŌĆö a policy engine over an attestation graph** ([`spec/trust-model.md`](spec/trust-model.md), [`tooling/validator/src/attestation.rs`](tooling/validator/src/attestation.rs), [`tooling/validator/src/policy.rs`](tooling/validator/src/policy.rs)). - **Richer trust policy ŌĆö confidence, recency, and true vertex-disjoint diversity** ([`spec/trust-model.md`](spec/trust-model.md), [`tooling/validator/src/policy.rs`](tooling/validator/src/policy.rs)). **Prover frontier.** The three named prover problems are now closed:

- **ŌæĀ Mutually-recursive equivalence** (`equiv`/`cluster`). Two **both-recursive** functions of **arity Ōēż 2** are decided by induction over the leading list parameter, with one **spectator** parameter threaded through both functions and **ŌłĆ-generalized in the induction hypothesis** ŌĆö the choice that closes both a *carried* spectator (append's second list, unchanged) and a *descending* one (zipWith's, tailed each step). The stride-targeted k-step search aligns constant-stride misalignments up to lcm 6 (incl. 2-vs-3); a both-recursive **step that needs a curated list-algebra lemma** (`append_nil`/`append_assoc`, each proved by its own induction before being assumed) closes too; and genuinely-unequal pairs are refuted by a short counterexample. `cluster` inherits all of this. *Higher-order list laws* also close now, including those needing an auxiliary lemma ŌĆö `map` laws over an uninterpreted function (`\f xs -> map(f, reverse xs) ŌēĪ \f xs -> reverse(map f xs)`, via `map_append`) and `filter`/`reverse` commutation (needs `filter_append` + `append_nil`) ŌĆö unblocked by an explicit e-matching **trigger** on each lemma's LHS plus a **minimal-subset search** when the full catalog set stalls z3's instantiation.
- **ŌæĪ Extended canonical normal form** (`normalize`). The meaning-preserving rewrite set now covers ╬▒-renaming, AC-ordering (incl. `min`/`max`), constant-folding, **subtraction-as-addition** (`a - b ŌåÆ a + (-b)`, negation distributed over sums), **idempotence** for `and`/`or`/`min`/`max`, and **polynomial normalization** ŌĆö `add`/`mul` are read as a polynomial `╬Ż cßĄó┬ĘmonomialßĄó + cŌéĆ` over their atoms, **products of sums are expanded**, and like monomials combined. So `x + x` ŌēĪ `2┬Ęx`, `(a+1)┬Ę(aŌłÆ1)` ŌēĪ `a┬Ęa ŌłÆ 1` (difference of squares), and `2┬Ę(a+b)` ŌēĪ `2┬Ęa + 2┬Ęb` share a normal form ŌĆö making `normalize` a **decision procedure for the integer polynomial fragment** (the distributivity AC ordering can't reach), so equivalences that needed z3 ŌĆö `double-via-add ŌēĪ double-via-mul`, or a both-recursive sum written `2*head` vs `head+head` ŌĆö now close **solver-free**. A handful of **sound list-algebra rewrites** join it ŌĆö `reverse(reverse x) ŌåÆ x`, `append(x, nil) ŌåÆ x` / `append(nil, x) ŌåÆ x`, `reverse(nil) ŌåÆ nil` ŌĆö so `reverse(reverse xs) ŌēĪ xs` (previously an inductive case) is now solver-free too; a *selector* like `head(cons h t) ŌåÆ h` is deliberately excluded (it drops `t`). Every rule is guarded by a reference-evaluator property test (over both the int/bool and list fragments); the polynomial form additionally **never drops an atom** (a monomial whose atom survives in no other term ŌĆö `x + (ŌłÆx)`, `a┬Ęb ŌłÆ a┬Ęb` ŌĆö aborts the rewrite rather than drop a possibly-diverging atom), so soundness holds by construction, not only by the value-level test.
- **Ōæó Beyond the size-ranked conjecture cap** (`explore`), via **relevance-guided promotion**. Raising the cap globally multiplies the per-conjecture induction cost on every goal (a measured 4├Ś regression), so instead exploration keeps the smallest-cap base untouched and *appends* a few conjectures from beyond it that share a non-trivial operator skeleton with the terms the goal equates (so they can fire there as a rewrite). Each is still proved by its own induction before use, so promotion only adds reach, never a false verdict.

**Still open (prover).** Equivalence/clustering of two mutually-recursive functions whose recursions **misalign beyond the targeted stride cap** (lcm > 240 ŌĆö the 2026-07-13 sweeps showed the single targeted attempt's cost is *linear* in the lcm with no cliff, so the cap is now purely a time budget; an *unreadable* stride still falls to the blind `1..12` search), or whose step needs a **cross-function lemma neither the curated catalog, accumulator-collapse discovery, nor the property path's new self-homomorphism discovery supplies** (theory exploration over the two-recursive step is blocked by z3 on nested-recursion `define-fun-rec`s ŌĆö a documented negative result, not a logic gap). **Arity is no longer a limit** (the generalized spectator IH is arity-uncapped ŌĆö `interleave3`/`interleave4` cons-vs-append prove in-house, and the per-element-algebra arity > 2 laws were always decided by normalization), and **reordered/interchangeable accumulators are no longer a limit** either (accumulator-collapse lemma discovery closes them ŌĆö two tail-accumulators threading the head into different accumulators now PROVE). (A cvc5 `--quant-ind` fallback was investigated for the arity > 2 gap and **reverted** ŌĆö once the in-house cap was lifted, cvc5 closed nothing the in-house prover + normalize don't already reach, and failed the same residuals: the recurring "cvc5 has no accessible reach over the full machinery" finding.) A **fuller list-algebra normal form** ŌĆö the integer polynomial fragment is a decision procedure, and the *sound local* list rewrites (reverse-involution, append-identity) are in, but the deeper list laws that require induction (`reverse(append xs ys) ŌēĪ append(reverse ys)(reverse xs)`, `filter`/`reverse` commutation, ŌĆ”) are reached only by the inductive prover, not by a confluent normal form. And the next lever for exploration's end-to-end reach, **relevance-guided fragment widening** (goal-seeded extra variables / larger terms, only for goal-relevant shapes ŌĆö affordable where a blind bump was 4├Ś), which still meets the same z3 ceiling on the harder goals it would reach.
